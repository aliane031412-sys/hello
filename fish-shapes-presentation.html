🐠 Fish Body & Tail Shapes 🐟

Explore how fish anatomy relates to their swimming behavior

Fish Body Shapes

Fusiform
🎯
Streamlined, torpedo-shaped body
Example: Tuna
Function: Fast swimming in open water
Compressiform
◀▶
Flattened from side to side
Example: Tautog
Function: Quick movement and turning for short distances
Depressiform
⬜
Flattened from top to bottom
Example: Skate
Function: Glides or swims close to the bottom like a flying bird
Filiform
〰️
Long, slender, thread-like body
Example: Pipefish
Function: Slithers through water like a snake

Fish Tail Shapes

Rounded
◯
Rounded tail shape
Strong acceleration & excellent maneuvering, but more drag and slower
Truncate
▬
Squared-off tail
Good acceleration and maneuverability
Emarginate
⌢
Slightly indented tail
Balances speed and maneuverability
Forked
⚡
Deeply divided tail
Good speed with less drag
Lunate
🌙
Crescent-shaped tail
Very fast, long-distance swimming but less maneuverable

💡 Easy Way to Remember:

ROUNDED → POWER (Acceleration & control, like a truck)

FORKED/LUNATE → SPEED (Fast & efficient, like a sports car)

TRUNCATE/EMARGINATE → BALANCED (All-purpose, like a sedan)
